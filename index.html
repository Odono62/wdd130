ebonyi state

Ebonyi State is a state in the **South-East of Nigeria**. It was created in **1996** from parts of Abia and Enugu States. The capital city is Abakaliki. The people of Ebonyi State are mainly **Igbo**, and they speak the **Igbo language**. The people are known to be hardworking and peaceful. Ebonyi State is known as the **“Salt of the Nation”** because of its natural **salt deposits**, especially in **Okposi** and **Uburu**. The state is also good for **farming**. Crops grown there include **rice, yam, cassava, and maize**. There are schools and higher institutions in the state, such as **Ebonyi State University**. The environment is mostly **rural** and calm, with fertile land. In summary, Ebonyi State is an important state in Nigeria because of its **agriculture, natural resources, and culture**.
