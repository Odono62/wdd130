My Homeland: Ebonyi State

Discover the beauty and richness of my home state in Nigeria

The "Salt of the Nation"

Ebonyi State is located in the South-East of Nigeria. It was created in 1996 from parts of Abia and Enugu States, with its capital in Abakaliki.

The people of Ebonyi State are predominantly Igbo, speaking the Igbo language. Known for their hardworking and peaceful nature, Ebonyi people contribute significantly to Nigeria's agricultural and economic sectors.

The state earns its nickname "Salt of the Nation" from its abundant natural salt deposits, particularly in Okposi and Uburu. These resources have been vital to the local economy for generations.
